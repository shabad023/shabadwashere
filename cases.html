CASE No. 001

The Overloaded Grid

SOLVED

THE SITUATION

Electric vehicle charging demand swings hard across hours and stations. Flat tariffs ignore it — overloading some stations, starving others, and leaving money and grid stability on the table.

THE INVESTIGATION

Built an agentic AI system on the ST-EVCDP and ACN-Data datasets: a three-agent architecture where a demand-prediction agent (Random Forest), a tariff-optimization agent, and a coordination agent negotiate station pricing dynamically.

FINDINGS

The system learned demand rhythms and adjusted tariffs to smooth load across stations — demonstrating that multi-agent pricing beats static tariffs on utilization balance.

INSTRUMENTS

Python · scikit-learn (Random Forest) · multi-agent architecture · ST-EVCDP · ACN-Data

CASE No. 002

The Volatile Surface

SOLVED

THE SITUATION

Options on the NIFTY50 imply a hidden surface — volatility across strikes and expiries. A Kaggle competition asked: predict its shape with minimal error.

THE INVESTIGATION

Developed a hybrid approach centered on PCHIP interpolation across the volatility surface, balancing smoothness against fidelity to observed market points.

FINDINGS

Final public leaderboard score ≈ 4.2e-5, ranking 71 — competitive performance from an interpretable, non-black-box method.

INSTRUMENTS

Python · pandas · scipy (PCHIP) · options/volatility fundamentals

CASE No. 003

The Price of Rain

SOLVED

THE SITUATION

Rideshare surge pricing earns more per trip but quietly damages demand. The question: where exactly does the damage begin, and which city forgives least?

THE INVESTIGATION

Analyzed 50,000 trips across Delhi, Bangalore, and Mumbai — segmenting demand response by surge multiplier, city, and context to isolate the inflection point.

FINDINGS

Demand damage concentrates between 1.5x and 1.75x surge — the critical inflection band. Bangalore proved the most churn-sensitive city of the three.

INSTRUMENTS

Python · pandas · exploratory analysis · pricing strategy framing

CASE No. 004

The Buried Lecture

SOLVED

THE SITUATION

Recorded lectures hold answers nobody can find — hours of video, no index, no way to ask. The task: make video lectures answer questions, with receipts.

THE INVESTIGATION

Built an end-to-end RAG pipeline: FFmpeg audio extraction → Whisper transcription → BGE-M3 embeddings via Ollama → Llama 3.2 generating answers with clickable timestamps, deployed on Streamlit.

FINDINGS

Ask a question, get the answer and the exact minute of the lecture it came from. Search time across hours of video drops to seconds.

INSTRUMENTS

Python · FFmpeg · Whisper · Ollama (BGE-M3) · Llama 3.2 · Streamlit
